binky masters

when bunnies jump straight up and twist in the air, it's called a "binky" ; their way of showing pure joy. Like your happy dance when you find a great vintage find.

rabbits can see nearly 360 degrees around them without moving their heads. the only blind spot is right in front of their nose. handy for spotting treats (or predators).

tooth trouble

a rabbit's teeth never stop growing. they wear them down by constantly chewing ! which explains why they'll nibble on anything from carrots to your favorite shoes.

speedy hoppers

the fastest rabbits can run up to 45 mph (i was surprised by this one) faster than most dogs. their zig-zag running pattern makes them even harder to catch when they're spooked.

social butterflies

in the wild, rabbits live in complex social groups called colonies. they communicate through subtle body language and even have a hierarchy like a secret scary little bunny society.
